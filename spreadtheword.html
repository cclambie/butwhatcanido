Why bother putting up a poster?

Because information alone doesn't change behaviour — but seeing something in the real world does.

🧠

It makes the abstract concrete

People understand "the ski run is turning to rain" far better than "global mean surface temperature anomaly." Local = real.

💬

It starts conversations

A striking image in a staffroom, café, or gym sparks questions. Questions lead to conversations. Conversations shift norms.

📱

The QR code does the work

Anyone who scans it lands on a page full of practical, tiered actions they can take today. The poster is just the door.

🌊

Social proof compounds

When people see others taking climate seriously, they're more likely to do the same. Visibility matters.

🏔️

It's about a place people love

Hotham isn't abstract. Millions of Australians have skied there, or know someone who has. That emotional connection is powerful.

🆓

Zero cost, zero barrier

Download, print, pin. That's it. No campaign to join, no donation required. Just a piece of paper doing quiet, persistent work.

Where does it work best?

High-dwell, high-relevance locations where people have a moment to stop and think.

🏢

Your workplace

Staffroom, kitchen, lift lobby, meeting room. People you already know will see it.

🏋️

Gym or sports club

Noticeboards, change rooms, reception areas. Active people often care about the outdoors.

☕

Cafés & restaurants

Ask the owner. Many are happy to support local environmental messaging.

🎿

Ski clubs & lodges

The most relevant audience of all. People who've watched the snow disappear over the years.

🏫

Schools & universities

Noticeboards, student unions, staff rooms. Young people are already engaged — give them something to point to.

📚

Libraries & community centres

Public noticeboards are designed for exactly this. No permission needed in most cases.

🏥

Medical waiting rooms

Captive audience with time to read. Climate is a health issue — many practices are receptive.

🏘️

Your neighbourhood

Local shops, community gardens, apartment building noticeboards. Start close to home.
